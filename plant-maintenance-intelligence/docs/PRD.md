# Product Requirements Document (PRD)

## Product
Plant Maintenance Intelligence

## Purpose
Build a demo-ready system that turns raw machine telemetry into actionable maintenance-risk intelligence directly inside Exasol, with a Streamlit dashboard for operators and evaluators.

## 1. Problem Statement & Customer Context
I am targeting an industrial scenario where rich machine telemetry—temperature, vibration, pressure, runtime hours, power draw, operating mode, and error codes—is collected from machines across multiple plants. Today, this data is often reviewed only after failures occur. My goal is to build a system that turns this raw telemetry into a ranked, actionable maintenance-risk intelligence feed.

### 1.1 The Gap Between Data and Action
Currently, engineers only investigate after a breakdown, treating all machines equally with no real prioritization. When a failure happens, the root cause is usually dug up manually, and the data is mostly locked in tables without being queried live. Maintenance happens on fixed calendar intervals regardless of actual machine wear.

To solve this, my target is to predict risk 24-72 hours before a threshold breach. I want to build a risk-ranked queue where the worst-performing machine is always visible at the top, showing exactly which sensor deviated to cause the flag. By keeping the scoring in-database, I eliminate the need for external ETLs and enable true condition-based servicing—doing maintenance only when the data says so.

### 1.2 Success Criteria for the Prototype
- **15-minute demo-ready**: Anyone evaluating my code can run it from a cold start.
- **All intelligence in Exasol**: No data moves to an external scoring service.
- **Explainable scores**: An ops engineer can read the number and understand why.
- **Credible mock data**: The patterns generated by my scripts closely mirror real industrial degradation trajectories.
- **Clear upgrade path**: Every decision I make in the prototype Maps to a direct production equivalent.

## 2. Risk Scoring & Business Value

### Risk Scoring Weights
For my scoring methodology, I've assigned specific weights based on signal importance:
- **Vibration (30%) & Temperature (25%)**: Measured via Z-scores compared to their baselines, as they are massive leading indicators of wear.
- **Pressure (20%) & Power Anomaly (10%)**: Measured as percentage deviations from the baseline.
- **Service Overdue (15%)**: This risk factor kicks in smoothly when a machine passes 80% of its scheduled service interval.
- **Error Codes (e.g., E5xx)**: These act as an urgent override, automatically adding a flat 0.25 premium (capped at a total score of 1.0) to ensure faults are flagged immediately.

Notes on calculation and capping:
- Each of the five components is normalized to a 0–1 contribution then multiplied by its weight and summed to produce a base risk score in the 0–1 range.
- `Vibration` and `Temperature` use Z-scores (current reading minus baseline divided by baseline stddev) that are passed through a short, monotonic curve to map extreme deviations into a 0–1 contribution.
- `Pressure` and `Power Anomaly` are computed as percentage deviations from baseline (e.g., (value - baseline) / baseline) and then scaled into a 0–1 contribution before applying their weights.
- `Service Overdue` begins to ramp from 0 once runtime reaches 80% of the scheduled service interval and increases smoothly toward 1.0 as it approaches the interval end.
- After summing the weighted components, apply the `Error Code` override: if an E5xx-style critical error is present, add a flat `+0.25` to the score. The final score is then clipped to the range `[0.0, 1.0]` so no reading can exceed `1.0`.

Example (simplified):
- Vibration contribution = 0.50 × 0.30 = 0.15
- Temperature contribution = 0.40 × 0.25 = 0.10
- Pressure contribution = 0.10 × 0.20 = 0.02
- Service overdue = 0.20 × 0.15 = 0.03
- Power anomaly = 0.00 × 0.10 = 0.00
- Base score = 0.30
- E5xx present → +0.25 → 0.55 (then clipped between 0.0 and 1.0)

### Risk Tiers & Actions
The resulting scores are bucketed into practical operational responses:
- **0.00 – 0.34 is LOW (Green)**: The machine can continue normal operation with no action required.
- **0.35 – 0.59 is MEDIUM (Amber)**: The machine requires closer monitoring, and an inspection should be booked within 72 hours.
- **0.60 – 0.79 is HIGH (Orange)**: Operaters should reduce the machine load and inspect within 24 hours.
- **0.80 – 1.00 is CRITICAL (Red)**: The machine must be halted immediately and the on-call engineer paged.

### Estimated Business Impact
Based on industry benchmarks from McKinsey and Deloitte, predictive maintenance typically yields a 20–40% reduction in unplanned downtime and a 10–25% reduction in maintenance costs. For my prototype, the immediate measurable win is taking fleet visibility from 0% to 100% by giving operators a live, ranked view of all machines.
